Experiments & Result

Experiments

The goal of this experiment is to verify if we could group players from their play data. We collected the play data from participants in an experiment. The data is then normalized and then fed into classification algorithms to group players from the most similar playing data.

3 healthy representatives of the laboratories are chosen from every 8 different laboratories at Ritsumeikan University, there are 24 participants in total. All 24 participants were asked to play the game once to collect the necessary data. Players must complete all 5 different levels from 1 to 5 in order. From the result shown in the Figure, Lazy IBk has the best accuracy to classify the data with an accuracy of 29.2% which is significantly better than chance level (12.5%).
